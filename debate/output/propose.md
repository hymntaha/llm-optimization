Large Language Models (LLMs) have become profoundly influential across society, impacting education, healthcare, media, and more. Their immense power to shape information means without strict laws regulating their development and deployment, they pose significant risks, including the spread of misinformation, manipulation of public opinion, invasion of privacy, and amplification of biases. Strict laws are essential to ensure transparency in how these models are trained and used, to protect individuals’ data, and to hold developers accountable for harmful outputs. Regulation will create enforceable standards to prevent misuse, encourage ethical innovation, and safeguard democratic processes. Without such frameworks, the uncontrolled proliferation of LLMs endangers trust, safety, and equity on a global scale. Therefore, strict laws are not just necessary—they are imperative for ensuring that LLMs serve humanity responsibly and fairly.
